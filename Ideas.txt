Right now, your XGBoost model is acting as a "Primary Model." It looks at the DOM, the velocity, and the Watchtower regime, and decides: "Will this trade win or lose?"

If you save all of these predictions—both the approved ones and the blocked ones—you can eventually train a Secondary Model (Meta-Model).

Model 1 (The Trigger): "I am 74% confident this is a buy."

Model 2 (The Risk Manager): Takes Model 1's confidence score, plus the current market volatility and time of day, and asks: "When Model 1 is 74% confident in Regime 1 at noon, is it usually right or wrong?"

The Two-Tier Sizing Engine
Right now, your dynamic sizing is a 1D curve. It looks strictly at Model 1 (The Primary Model):

Model 1 says: "I am 85% confident this velocity spike will revert."

Sizing Engine says: "85% confidence hits our exponential curve. Fire 4.0 lots."

When you introduce Model 2 (The Meta-Model), you upgrade to a 2D risk matrix. Model 2 does not care about the velocity spike; it only cares about whether Model 1 is currently hallucinating based on the macro environment.

Model 2 says: "Model 1 just fired an 85% confidence signal. However, it is 9:45 AM, the 1H ATR is expanding aggressively, and Model 1 historically loses 70% of its trades in this specific environment. I am only 30% confident Model 1 is right."
